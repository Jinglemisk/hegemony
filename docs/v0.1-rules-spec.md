# Hegemony Current Rules Spec

> **Authority note:** this document has drifted from the implementation and is out of date in places. Where it conflicts with the engine (`src/game/`), **the engine is the source of truth**. Known differences the code intentionally keeps: capital pop capacity is 10 (not 20), capital tile income is not doubled, and capital building-slot bonus is +2 (not +4). Balance values now live in the `Ruleset` (`src/game/ruleset.ts`); modes patch it via `GAME_MODES`.

This is the implementation contract for the current browser prototype. It deliberately excludes many systems from the archive rules so the game can become playable before the design is complete.

## Goal

Build a hotseat browser prototype with a real rules engine and a small local turn controller. The prototype proves the core loop:

1. Place capital.
2. Place colony.
3. Start season.
4. Collect income.
5. Found new colonies.
6. Upgrade colonies into cities.
7. Build basic buildings.
8. End turn.

Online multiplayer, events, assembly voting, national ideas, luxury goods, and final victory conditions are deferred.

## Technology

- TypeScript for rules and UI.
- React plus Vite for the browser client.
- Local React state for the hotseat turn controller.
- SVG for the first map renderer.

## Player Count

The current prototype supports 4 seats in hotseat mode.

The game should be written so 2 to 4 players can be supported later without rewriting the rules.

## Map

The current prototype uses one inland hex board:

- Radius 3 axial hex grid.
- 37 terrain tiles.
- No coastal tiles yet.
- No ports yet.
- No luxury goods yet.

The terrain deck uses the PDF's counts:

- 6 mountain.
- 9 hill.
- 14 forest.
- 8 plains.

The hill distribution is a temporary completion because the PDF does not fully specify all 9 hill tiles.

## Phases

### setupCapital

Each player places one capital.

Rules:

- Must place on an empty tile.
- Cannot place adjacent to another city or capital.
- One move ends the player's setup turn.

### setupColony

Each player places one colony.

Rules:

- Cannot place on a tile with a city or capital.
- Cannot place adjacent to an enemy capital.
- A tile may contain at most 2 colonies.
- A player cannot have two colonies on the same tile.
- One move ends the player's setup turn.

### gameplay

Each player's turn:

1. Income is collected automatically once when the gameplay turn begins.
2. Optionally found colonies, upgrade a colony to a city, Grow Pop, or build basic buildings.
3. End turn.

Season starts before player 1's turn. When play returns to player 1, a new season starts, turn income and settlement growth flags reset, and player 1's income is collected automatically.

## Settlements

### Capital

Capital rules:

- Counts as a city.
- Collects double tile resource income.
- Has 20 pop capacity.
- Has terrain building slots plus 4.
- During setup, the placing player allocates exactly 3 starting pops across citizens, freemen, and slaves.

### Colony

Colony rules:

- Has 4 pop capacity.
- Has no building slots.
- Can share a tile with one enemy colony.
- Shared colony tile income is halved for both players.
- During setup, the placing player allocates exactly 1 starting pop across citizens, freemen, and slaves.

Gameplay colony founding:

- Costs 20 wood and 2 food.
- Cannot be placed on a tile with a city or capital.
- Cannot be placed adjacent to an enemy capital.
- A tile may contain at most 2 colonies.
- A player cannot have two settlements on the same tile.

### City

City upgrade:

- Costs 30 wood, 10 stone, and 5 food.
- Requires the current player's colony on the tile.
- Cannot upgrade on a tile that already has a city or capital.
- Cannot create a city adjacent to any other city or capital.
- 10 pop capacity.
- Terrain building slots plus 2.
- Preserves the upgraded colony's pops and buildings.
- Removes any enemy colony sharing the upgraded tile.

## Resources

Tracked resources:

- Wood.
- Stone.
- Gold.
- Food.
- Influence.
- Happiness.

Starting resources:

- 20 wood.
- 10 stone.
- 10 gold.
- 12 food.
- 0 influence.
- 0 happiness.

These are generous because the current prototype needs fast building and upgrade tests.

## Population

Tracked pop types:

- Citizens.
- Freemen.
- Slaves.

The prototype uses normalized pop-level units. One displayed citizen, freeman, or slave each counts as one population capacity unit:

- 1 citizen pop = 1 capacity.
- 1 freeman pop = 1 capacity.
- 1 slave pop = 1 capacity.

The older event conversion `1 citizen = 2 freemen = 3 slaves` is no longer used for income or capacity in the prototype.

Income:

- Each citizen gives +1 influence and -2 food.
- Each freeman gives +2 gold and -1 food.
- Each slave gives +1 of the tile's primary resource, -1 food, and -0.5 happiness.

Grow Pop action:

- The active player chooses one owned settlement and one pop type to add.
- Slave costs 5 food.
- Freeman costs 7 food.
- Citizen costs 9 food and 2 gold.
- A settlement can Grow Pop once per owner turn.
- The action is illegal if the settlement is at population capacity or the player cannot afford the adjusted cost.
- Each local granary reduces the food part of the Grow Pop cost by 2, to a minimum of 0 food.

Food shortage:

- Projected food stockpile equals current player food plus projected food income.
- Food-shortage happiness pressure is applied only when projected food stockpile is below 0.
- The pressure equals the projected food stockpile, so a projected stockpile of -3 applies -3 happiness.
- Each player's first gameplay income collection after setup ignores food-shortage happiness pressure.
- Other happiness effects still apply on that first gameplay income collection.
- The two-consecutive-turn starvation rule is documented but deferred.

Capacity pressure:

- Each settlement has a population capacity.
- Capitals have 20 capacity.
- Cities have 10 capacity.
- Colonies have 4 capacity.
- During income collection, each population above capacity adds -1 happiness.

## Buildings

The current prototype implements only the basic buildings needed to test city growth and population pressure.

### Marketplace

Cost:

- 12 wood.

Effect:

- Adds +2 additional gold per freeman.
- Each Marketplace supports up to 3 freemen.
- Multiple Marketplaces stack their supported freemen capacity.

### Temple

Cost:

- 6 stone.

Effect:

- Adds +1 happiness.
- Adds +1 additional influence per citizen.
- Each Temple supports up to 2 citizens for the influence bonus.
- Multiple Temples stack their supported citizen capacity.

### Workshop

Cost:

- 12 wood.

Effect:

- Adds +1 tile primary resource per slave.
- Each Workshop supports up to 3 slaves.
- Multiple Workshops stack their supported slave capacity.

### Granary

Cost:

- 12 wood.
- 2 stone.

Effect:

- Adds +2 food income.
- Local Grow Pop costs -2 food.

## Deferred Systems

- Player event cards.
- Public event cards.
- Resolution cards.
- Hegemony Assembly.
- Politicians.

- Trade.
- Repeal and veto.
- Victory conditions.
- AI or bots.
- Persistent accounts.
- Remote multiplayer deployment.

## Implementation Rules

The rules engine should stay separate from the React UI.

The React UI may:

- Render state.
- Let players select actions.
- Show legal/illegal feedback.
- Call shared rules functions through the local turn controller.

The React UI must not:

- Own permanent game state.
- Apply resource changes directly.
- Decide whether an action is legal.
- Duplicate costs or production formulas outside shared data.
