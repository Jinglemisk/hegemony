02

The Influence-Sink Loop

citizens → influence → political power → resolutions → back to the citizen line

Read it as one motion: citizens generate influence; the Assembly is the one place that influence is spent to buy political power; that power enacts resolutions; resolutions bend the economy back toward the citizen line — which makes more citizens. The whole point of the Assembly is to close the terracotta edge. Until it ships, the citizen line can't pay, and the smart bot has nothing to spend on.

The Eight Open Questions

§5 · the actual decision list · lean and jot on each
Q1 · Flow
The moment-to-moment flow under choose-not-draw?
Do all players see the 4 menus and propose/select one (paying influence), then the Assembly votes on the set? Or does selecting == enacting, gated purely by cost — no yay/nay? This decides whether "votes" even matter.

Lean

Q2 · Verbs
So what are the influence verbs now?
Draw / play / exchange are stale — they presume a drawn hand. Candidate choose-model verbs: propose onto the ballot · add votes (weight your yay/nay) · veto (once) · propose-repeal. Pin the set and its costs — that IS the sink.

Lean

Q3 · Politician layer
How thin is the politician layer in v1?
Ratify or amend the rec: power track + patron buff + leader-check; no politician victory, no danger track. Each passed resolution → +1 power to its politician; top contributor is patron. Define each patron buff on existing levers.

Lean

Q4 · Stratokles
How does Stratokles's leader-check fire and resolve?
Pot mechanics: who pays, how much hits the leader, how often it can appear. This is the anti-runaway valve — get it right or it reads as bash-the-leader. The victory-card leader is already computed (tie→null) for it to target.

Lean

Q5 · Runaway
Vote scaling & political runaway.
If votes scale with citizens, the economic leader is also the political leader. Decide the vote curve / cap, and whether political victory ("double everyone's votes") ships in v1 at all — it could snowball.

Lean

Q6 · Cadence sanity
Is the Year-3 slow burn intended?
First Assembly at Year 3 means ~half a typical game (race wins land ~yr 4.8–5) has no political layer. Confirm the slow burn is correct, or pull it to Year 2 so the payoff doesn't feel bolted-on.

Lean

Q7 · Surface
How is the "mode over the map" mounted?
Full-screen surface: engine-state mount (like the omen, on a year≠seen gate) or player-triggered (an ActiveModal kind)? Confirm it rides the ModalShell dial + route model, and lands the deferred dossier / deep-links with it.

Lean

Q8 · Bot voting
Where does bot voting land?
v1 is hand-playtested — bots have no notion of influence value, votes or resolution utility. The eventual influence-aware bot (Phase 3-C) needs voting/bidding logic. Note it as the follow-on so v1's design doesn't make it impossible.

Pros & Cons

§3 · the trade-off ledger, verbatim in substance

Pros

  • Fills a proven balance hole — the sink the citizen line needs to pay, not a speculative one.
  • Rivalry-native — the first mechanic where players directly contest each other; checks runaway leaders through play.
  • Thin engine layer by construction — resolutions are data over the event-table law; effects are Ruleset patches; the new-year hook, leader query and pop-scaling helper all already exist.
  • High agency / low variance (choose-not-draw) — rewards skill, dodges bad-draw complaints, legible for deep-links.
  • Rich thematic payoff — the Athenian ecclesia, named politicians, ostracism-as-leader-check; fully aligned with the Greek frame and reserved Aegean-blue politics language.
  • Unlocks the deferred UI — the dossier, deep-links (two-panel 4–5) and the drawn urn icon finally get a home.

Cons & risks

  • Deterministic menus are a balance minefield — no shuffle, so the strongest resolution gets picked every game; balance rests entirely on friction.
  • The pivot orphans the drafted verbs — draw / play / exchange presume a hand; the verb set must be re-derived under choose-from-menus.
  • Vote–citizen coupling can create a second runaway — if votes scale with citizens, the economic leader is the political leader too; a political-victory threshold could snowball.
  • The AI can't validate it yet — bots have no notion of influence value or votes; v1 must be hand-playtested before the sim can speak.
  • Persistent state accumulates — "active until repealed" resolutions + a growing power track = more standing modifiers to surface and keep legible.
  • Leader-bashing feel — Stratokles's pooled pot can read as punishing whoever's ahead if it fires too easily or hits too hard.
  • UI density stays the flat grammar's weak point; the full-screen mode is the answer on paper but genuinely new surface to get right.
  • Cadence delay — Year-3 first Assembly means half a game with no political layer: correct slow burn, or bolted-on?
