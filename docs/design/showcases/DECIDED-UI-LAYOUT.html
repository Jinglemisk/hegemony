The circle law

Discs are verbs and menus — things you press, repeatedly. The one square ends the turn. Shape carries the grammar, so the commit can never be misfired for a verb.

Light-mode parchment

Per the standing ruling the game is light parchment; the black glaze survives only as the ceremony register — tooltips, key chips, and the commit. The sea is now the one dark value, so anything floating on it (season, whose-turn) stays ivory.

Spines (A) vs domes (B/C)

Discs are always threaded on an edge, ~3/5 out over the sea. In A that edge is a straight 30px spine; in B/C it is the arc of an ellipse, so the verbs fan — apex at centre, lower at the rim. A and C differ only in that shape.

Why an ellipse, not a circle

Seven verbs need ~630px of span; a true circle only reaches that at its equator, where the arc runs vertical — the fan is geometrically impossible. And the fan is 744px wide with its labels vs the row's 446: the verbs size the dome, not the resources. Dead space under the apex is exactly ry − 73, so depth is the only dial.
