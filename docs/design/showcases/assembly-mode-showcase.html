THE ASSEMBLY — Hegemony · design showcase Phase

Sized to the sea

The Assembly is not a full-screen takeover — it floats in the sea gap between the ledger and the right edge. The top bar, rail, ledger, dock and the board all stay live around it, so the panel never re-states resources, influence, the roster or the season.

Laws are stelae

Every passed Law is inscribed on a stele stacked beneath its politician. Stack height = power; the dominant author colour = the patron. No hidden counters — the board is the scoreboard.

Votes cost economy

Each pebble on the band is a player: its number is their citizen count, which is their vote weight. Citizens cost upkeep and happiness, so the loudest voice is rarely the richest. Influence buys draws, bribes, vetoes, repeals.

Stratokles & the coup

His cards are one-time populist Directives — riots that hit the whole table. Each leaves a tally monument; at 3 the demagogue seizes the city and his patron wins.
